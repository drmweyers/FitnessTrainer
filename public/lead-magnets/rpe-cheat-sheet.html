RPE Cheat Sheet
Rate of Perceived Exertion — The trainer's intensity guide

RPE measures how hard an exercise feels on a scale of 1–10. Use it to prescribe intensity without a 1RM test. Pair with Reps In Reserve (RIR) for precision programming.

RIR = Reps left in the tank
Zones: 1–4 Recovery / Easy 5–6 Light / Moderate 7–8 Hard / Heavy 9–10 Very Hard / Max
RPE Zone How It Feels Example / Programming Use RIR
1
Rest
No effort at all
Complete rest. No physical exertion. Lying still or seated comfortably. Breathing is totally normal.
Use for:
Rest intervals between max-effort sets. Not typically prescribed in programs.
∞
unlimited reps left
2
Very Light
Barely moving
Minimal effort. Slow walking pace. Could sustain this for hours. No real challenge to breathing or muscles.
Example:
Easy walk, mobility drills, warm-up foam rolling, gentle stretching.
∞
unlimited reps left
3
Light
Easy, comfortable
Slight exertion. Can hold a full conversation. Breathing slightly elevated. Used for warm-up sets.
Example:
Warm-up sets at 40–50% 1RM. Light band work. Technique practice with empty bar.
8+
many reps left
4
Somewhat Easy
Noticeable but easy
Starting to feel the work. Breathing picks up slightly. Still very comfortable — form is perfect, no real challenge.
Example:
Feeder sets at 55–65% 1RM. Deload week loads. Corrective work.
6–7
many reps left
5
Moderate
Comfortable effort
Effort is clear. Can still talk in sentences. Breathing is elevated but not laboured. Could maintain this for many more reps.
Example:
Hypertrophy warm-up sets. Conditioning circuits. 65–70% 1RM loads.
5–6
several reps left
6
Somewhat Hard
Challenging, manageable
Noticeably challenging. Short sentences only. Muscles working steadily. Form is still crisp. Endurance athletes often train here.
Example:
Hypertrophy working sets (early). Accessory lifts. 70–75% 1RM.
4–5
a few reps left
7
Hard
Uncomfortable, doable
Genuinely hard. Single words only. Muscles burning noticeably. Starting to think about stopping — but you wouldn't. Standard hypertrophy zone.
Example:
Main hypertrophy sets. 3–4 reps before failure. ~75–80% 1RM.
3–4
3–4 reps left
8
Very Hard
Heavy, demanding
Very hard. Can barely speak. Muscles significantly fatigued. Would struggle to add another rep without grinding hard. Strength training zone.
Example:
Strength top sets. 2 reps before failure. ~80–87.5% 1RM.
2–3
2–3 reps left
9
Near Max
Almost everything
Near maximal. Talking is impossible. Grinding hard. One rep left — maybe. Bar speed is slow. Requires full mental focus and bracing.
Example:
Peaking sets. 1 rep before failure. ~87.5–95% 1RM. Competition prep.
1
1 rep left
10
Max Effort
Absolute limit
100% effort. True maximum. Could not have done another rep under any circumstances. Bar speed is extremely slow or grinding.
Example:
1RM attempts. Competition max-outs. True technical failure sets.
0
nothing left

Pro Tip: New clients consistently over-estimate their RPE by 1–2 points. Calibrate in the first 2–3 sessions by asking "how many more reps could you have done?" If they say 5+ but rated it RPE 8, recalibrate. RPE is a skill — teach it early.
