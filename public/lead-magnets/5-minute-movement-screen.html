Instructions: Perform each test in order. Grade each movement as PASS or FAIL. Note any compensations, pain reports, or asymmetries. Complete all 6 tests before programming. Time: approximately 4–5 minutes total.

1
Overhead Squat
Ankle · Hip · Thoracic Mobility
✓ PASS CRITERIA
  • Hips descend to or below parallel
  • Knees track over toes (no collapse)
  • Torso stays upright (<45° forward lean)
  • Arms remain overhead throughout
  • Heels maintain floor contact
    • ✗ FAIL CRITERIA
  • Heels lift off floor
  • Knees cave inward (valgus)
  • Excessive forward torso lean
  • Arms fall forward significantly
  • Unable to reach parallel depth
    • ⚡ MODIFICATION / NOTE
  • Elevate heels 1–2 inches to screen ankle mobility separately
  • If arms fall: assess thoracic rotation
  • Knee valgus: assess hip abductor strength
    • PASS
    FAIL
    Notes:
    2
    Single-Leg Balance
    Proprioception · Ankle Stability
    ✓ PASS CRITERIA
  • Eyes open: 30s each side without touching down
  • Eyes closed: 15s each side stable
  • Minimal trunk sway
  • No notable L/R asymmetry
    • ✗ FAIL CRITERIA
  • Unable to hold 30s eyes open
  • Repeated toe-touches or hops
  • Significant side-to-side difference (>5s)
  • Excessive arm-windmilling to stabilise
    • ⚡ MODIFICATION / NOTE
  • Start with hands on hips to remove arm compensation
  • Asymmetry >5s: screen for previous ankle/knee injury
  • Progress: narrow stance → eyes closed
    • PASS
    FAIL
    Notes:
    3
    Hip Hinge (Toe Touch)
    Hamstring Length · Posterior Chain
    ✓ PASS CRITERIA
  • Fingertips reach floor or within 3 inches
  • Spine maintains natural S-curve
  • Hips drive back, not down
  • Knees soft (not locked out)
    • ✗ FAIL CRITERIA
  • Excessive spinal rounding (thoracic kyphosis)
  • Cannot reach past mid-shin
  • Knee hyperextension or extreme knee bend
  • Pain or pulling in lower back
    • ⚡ MODIFICATION / NOTE
  • Place dowel rod along spine: contact at head, thoracic, sacrum
  • Can't hinge without rounding: regress to box hip hinge
  • Tight hamstrings: prioritise Romanian DL with light load
    • PASS
    FAIL
    Notes:
    4
    Push-Up Plank Hold
    Shoulder Stability · Core Control
    ✓ PASS CRITERIA
  • Hold push-up position 10s stable
  • Body in straight line (ankle-shoulder)
  • Scapulae flat, no winging
  • Hips level — no sag or piking
    • ✗ FAIL CRITERIA
  • Scapular winging visible
  • Hips drop or hike within 10s
  • Unable to maintain without trembling/collapse
  • Shoulder pain or clicking
    • ⚡ MODIFICATION / NOTE
  • Regress to knees if unable to hold toes
  • Winging: address serratus anterior before heavy pressing
  • Hip sag: prioritise anterior core work in Phase 1
    • PASS
    FAIL
    Notes:
    5
    Active Straight Leg Raise
    Hip Flexion · Hamstring Length
    ✓ PASS CRITERIA
  • Raise leg to 90° hip flexion (vertical)
  • Pelvis stays flat/neutral on floor
  • Down leg stays fully extended
  • No spinal rotation or lower back lift
    • ✗ FAIL CRITERIA
  • Leg stops below 70° without compensation
  • Pelvis tilts or rotates during raise
  • Opposite leg bends or externally rotates
  • Lower back lifts from floor
    • ⚡ MODIFICATION / NOTE
  • Place hand under lumbar to monitor spine position
  • Below 70°: focus on passive hamstring stretching before RDLs
  • Asymmetry: note L/R separately — flag for follow-up
    • PASS (Left)
    FAIL (Left)

    6
    Shoulder Mobility (Apley Scratch)
    Internal / External Rotation · Lat Tightness
    ✓ PASS CRITERIA
  • Both hands touch or come within 2 inches
  • Hands: one reaching over shoulder, one behind lower back
  • No pain, clicking, or impingement symptoms
  • Symmetrical range both sides
    • ✗ FAIL CRITERIA
  • Hands more than 4 inches apart
  • Pain or sharp discomfort at end range
  • Significant L/R asymmetry (>2 inches difference)
  • Thoracic rotation compensation to reach
    • ⚡ MODIFICATION / NOTE
  • Asymmetry or pain: defer overhead pressing; assess rotator cuff
  • Tight lats: add doorway stretching before vertical pull work
  • Both sides: test and record measurement each side

    PROGRAMMING GUIDE
    Page 3 of 3
    Step 1 — Tally Your Score
    Count the number of PASS results from Page 2. Each test = 1 point. Maximum score: 6/6.
    5–6 / 6 Ready to Train Excellent movement quality. Client is cleared for progressive loading. Address any single failing test with targeted accessory work. Prioritise strength and conditioning goals.
    3–4 / 6 Foundation Phase Notable mobility or stability restrictions present. Begin with a 2–4 week corrective phase focusing on failing tests before introducing significant external load.
    0–2 / 6 Corrective First Multiple significant restrictions. Implement a dedicated corrective program (4–6 weeks). Consider physiotherapy referral if pain was reported on any test. Re-screen before loading.
    Step 2 — Programming Recommendations
    ▲
    Overhead Squat FAIL
    • Add ankle mobility drills (90–90 stretches, wall ankle mobs) daily
    • Begin goblet squat progression with heel elevation
    • Include thoracic extension over foam roller
    • Defer barbell back squat until screen passes
    ◈
    Single-Leg Balance FAIL
    • Daily single-leg standing practice (2×30s each side)
    • Add BOSU balance work and single-leg RDL with light load
    • Avoid bilateral lower body loading as primary stimulus
    • Screen for previous ankle sprains or knee injuries
    ↓
    Hip Hinge FAIL
    • Teach hip hinge pattern using wall or box as tactile cue
    • Begin Romanian DL with light dumbbells (focus on form)
    • Add hamstring flexibility work: lying stretch, PNF
    • Hold deadlift variations until hinge pattern grooves correctly
    ■
    Push-Up Plank FAIL
    • Prioritise anterior core: dead bugs, hollow holds, ab wheel
    • Address scapular winging with serratus wall slides
    • Regress pressing to incline push-up until plank is stable
    • Include band pull-aparts to improve shoulder stability
