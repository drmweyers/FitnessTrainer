Upper Body — Push & Pull
Use these swaps when equipment is unavailable, technique breaks down, or the client has upper body limitations
Primary Exercise Alternative 1 Alternative 2 Alternative 3 Why Substitute Difficulty
Barbell Bench Press
Primary: Chest · Triceps · Anterior Delt
  • Dumbbell Press
    Greater ROM, independent arm movement, easier shoulder positioning
  • Push-Ups (weighted or elevated)
    No equipment; add load via vest or deficit for progression
  • Cable Chest Flyes
    Constant tension, reduced shoulder impingement risk, good isolation
 Shoulder pain, no barbell access, beginner clients, rotator cuff history EASIER
Pull-Ups / Chin-Ups
Primary: Lats · Biceps · Rear Delt
  • Lat Pulldown
    Adjustable load, same movement pattern, ideal for beginners
  • Band-Assisted Pull-Up
    Builds confidence on the bar, maintains full ROM
  • Inverted Row (TRX / Bar)
    Horizontal pulling pattern, scales easily with body angle
 Insufficient strength, shoulder impingement, no pull-up bar access EASIER
Overhead Press (BB)
Primary: Anterior Delt · Triceps · Upper Trap
  • Landmine Press
    Arc path reduces shoulder impingement; great for beginners
  • Arnold Press (DB)
    Adds rotational component, hits all three delt heads
  • Pike Push-Ups
    No equipment, trains same pattern, scales with elevation angle
 Overhead mobility restriction, shoulder impingement, no barbell access SIMILAR
Barbell Row
Primary: Lats · Rhomboids · Rear Delt · Biceps
  • Seated Cable Row
    Constant tension, lower back stress reduced, easy load management
  • Single-Arm DB Row
    Unilateral loading, addresses asymmetries, supports lower back
  • TRX / Ring Row
    Bodyweight option, core activated, easily adjusted by angle
 Lower back pain, no barbell, hip hinge limitation, beginner clients SIMILAR
Trainer Tip: When substituting, preserve the primary muscle group target — not just the movement pattern. A horizontal push targets chest regardless of whether it's a barbell, dumbbell, or body-weight variation.
Quick Reference: Difficulty Ratings
EASIER Lower skill/load demand — good for beginners or deload phases
SIMILAR Same relative demand — direct swap without adjusting intensity
HARDER Increased demand — use as progression once original is mastered

Lower Body — Squat, Hinge & Lunge Patterns
Maintain lower body stimulus when primary exercises are unavailable or the client has knee, hip, or lower back restrictions
Primary Exercise Alternative 1 Alternative 2 Alternative 3 Why Substitute Difficulty
Back Squat (BB)
Primary: Quads · Glutes · Core · Hamstrings
  • Goblet Squat
    Anterior load teaches upright torso; perfect for technique correction
  • Leg Press
    Removes spinal loading; high quad activation; safe for back pain
  • Bulgarian Split Squat
    Unilateral; greater glute activation; addresses L/R asymmetry
 Lower back pain, poor bar position tolerance, limited mobility, beginner EASIER
Conventional Deadlift
Primary: Hamstrings · Glutes · Erectors · Traps
  • Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
    Removes floor pull; emphasises hamstring stretch under load
  • Trap Bar Deadlift
    Neutral grip; reduced spinal shear; easier to maintain form
  • Hip Thrust (BB / DB)
    Maximal glute activation; no spinal loading; knee-friendly
 Lower back injury, grip strength deficit, inability to hold neutral spine from floor SIMILAR
Barbell Lunge
Primary: Quads · Glutes · Hip Flexors · Balance
  • Step-Ups (DB / BB)
    Removed balance challenge; greater glute emphasis; height adjustable
  • Static Split Squat
    Static base removes walking component; great for knee stability work
  • Reverse Lunge (DB)
    Reduces anterior knee shear; preferred for clients with knee pain
 Knee pain, balance deficit, no barbell, limited space, walking gait issues SIMILAR
Trainer Tip: Maintain the same weekly volume (sets × reps × load) when substituting. If using an easier variation, slightly increase sets or reps to preserve training stimulus.
Injury-Specific Substitution Rules
Lower Back Pain
→ Replace conventional DL with RDL or hip thrust
→ Replace back squat with leg press or goblet squat
→ Avoid spinal compression movements
Knee Pain / Patellofemoral
→ Replace lunges with reverse lunges or step-ups
→ Reduce squat depth or use leg press
→ Prioritise hip thrust for quad alternative
Shoulder Impingement
→ Replace overhead press with landmine press
→ Replace barbell row with cable row
→ Use neutral grip where possible

No Equipment (Bodyweight Only)
Zero equipment required · Perfect for home workouts or travel
Chest / Push
  • Push-Ups (standard, wide, narrow)
  • Decline Push-Ups (feet elevated)
  • Archer Push-Ups (unilateral load)
  • Pike Push-Ups (shoulder focus)
Back / Pull
  • Doorframe Row
  • Inverted Row (table edge)
  • Superman Hold (posterior chain)
  • Scapular Wall Slides
Legs / Lower Body
  • Bodyweight Squat → Jump Squat
  • Reverse Lunge
  • Single-Leg Hip Thrust (floor)
  • Nordic Hamstring Curl
Shoulders
  • Pike Push-Ups (progression)
  • Handstand Hold (wall-supported)
  • Y/T/W Raises (prone)
  • Band Pull-Aparts (substitute)
Core / Stability
  • Plank → Side Plank
  • Dead Bug
  • Hollow Body Hold
  • Bird Dog
Conditioning
  • Burpees
  • Mountain Climbers
  • Jump Lunges
  • Bear Crawl

Dumbbells Only
Home gym or limited gym setup · Covers all major movement patterns

  • DB Shoulder Press
  • DB Chest Fly (floor)
  • Renegade Row (push + pull)
Pull (Back / Biceps)
  • Single-Arm DB Row
  • DB Bent-Over Row (bilateral)
  • DB Bicep Curl → Hammer Curl

Lower Body
  • DB Goblet Squat
  • DB Romanian Deadlift
  • DB Bulgarian Split Squat
  • DB Hip Thrust (bench or floor)

  • Banded Monster Walk
